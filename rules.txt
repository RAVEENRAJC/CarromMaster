A turn begins when the player places the striker and ends when the striker comes to rest.

Only one hand may be used to strike the striker.

The striker must be placed on or within the baseline area before every shot.

The striker must not be placed touching the arrows or touching a coin.

The striker must be struck only by flicking; pushing the striker is not allowed.

A player may strike with the index finger, middle finger, thumb, or any legal flicking technique.

The striker must move forward, not backward. Backward strike is a foul.

The striker must not be dragged or pushed; it must be released cleanly.

The striker must touch at least one coin to count as a legal shot.

If the striker fails to touch any coin, it is a foul.

If the striker leaves the board, it is a foul.

The striker ends the turn immediately if it is pocketed.

The striker may not touch an opponent’s coin intentionally.

If the striker pockets an opponent’s coin, the coin is returned to the board and a penalty applies.

If the striker pockets the striker itself, it is a foul and one previously pocketed coin must be returned.

If the striker is pocketed while the player also pockets their coin, the pocketed coin stays but the foul penalty still applies.

If a player pockets the striker after pocketing the last coin, the last coin is returned and foul applies.

If a player pockets the queen, they must cover it by pocketing one of their own coins in the same or immediate next turn.

If the queen is not covered, it is returned to the center of the board.

If the last coin is pocketed without covering the queen, the queen is returned to the center.

If the queen is pocketed before any coin of the player is pocketed, the queen is returned.

If the queen is pocketed and covered in the same shot, it is valid.

If the queen is pocketed after the last coin, it is considered uncovered and returns to the board.

If a player pockets the opponent’s coin, the opponent’s coin is returned to the board.

If a player pockets their own coin and opponent’s coin together, the opponent’s coin is returned.

A break shot starts the game.

During break, at least one coin must cross the baseline or touch a pocket; otherwise it is a foul.

If the striker is pocketed on break, foul applies.

If the striker touches the queen during break, it is not a foul.

Black and white coins must be arranged symmetrically in the circle.

If a player commits multiple fouls in one shot, only one penalty coin is returned.

If the striker is pocketed and the player also knocks a coin off the board, both penalties apply.

A player cannot waste time by repeated re-positioning of the striker.

The opponent may appeal to the referee about time violation.

Shots must be taken cleanly without shaking the board.

Coins near the pocket must not be pushed intentionally; they must be struck legally.
